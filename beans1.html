Coffee Bean Types

Coffee is more than just a drink—it's a world of flavor, culture, and craftsmanship.
While most people are familiar with "Arabica," there are four major coffee bean types that define the global coffee industry:

Arabica, Robusta, Liberica, and Excelsa.

Each has its own story, aroma, caffeine level, and growing region—making every cup a unique journey.

1. Arabica Coffee

The most popular type in the world—representing 60–70% of global production. Grown in high-altitude regions like Latin America and East Africa.

  • Shape: Oval and slightly elongated.
  • Taste: Smooth, sweet, and mildly acidic.
  • Caffeine: Lower compared to others.
  • Aroma: Fruity, floral, sometimes nutty or cocoa-like.
  • Best Brewing: V60, Chemex, French Press, AeroPress.
