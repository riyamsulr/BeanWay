Coffee Bean Types

Coffee is more than just a drink—it's a world of flavor, culture, and craftsmanship.
While most people are familiar with "Arabica," there are four major coffee bean types that define the global coffee industry:

Arabica, Robusta, Liberica, and Excelsa.

Each has its own story, aroma, caffeine level, and growing region—making every cup a unique journey.

1. Arabica Coffee

The most popular type in the world—representing 60–70% of global production. Grown in high-altitude regions like Latin America and East Africa.

  • Shape: Oval and slightly elongated.
  • Taste: Smooth, sweet, and mildly acidic.
  • Caffeine: Lower compared to others.
  • Aroma: Fruity, floral, sometimes nutty or cocoa-like.
  • Best Brewing: V60, Chemex, French Press, AeroPress.

2. Robusta Coffee

The second most common variety, cultivated mainly in Africa and Southeast Asia. Known for its bold flavor and high caffeine content—almost twice that of Arabica.

  • Shape: Rounder and smaller.
  • Taste: Strong, bitter, with earthy or chocolate notes.
  • Caffeine: Very high.
  • Best Brewing: Espresso, Cappuccino, Latte blends.

3. Liberica Coffee

A rare and distinctive coffee bean grown mostly in the Philippines and Malaysia. It offers a floral, smoky flavor with a unique woody aftertaste.

  • Taste: Floral, fruity, slightly woody.
  • Caffeine: Moderate.
  • Aroma: Bold and complex.
  • Best Brewing: Manual Brew, Cold Brew, Medium Roast.

4. Excelsa Coffee

A unique variant of the Liberica family, Excelsa adds depth and fruitiness to coffee blends. Mainly grown in Southeast Asia, especially Vietnam and the Philippines.

  • Shape: Small, teardrop-like beans.
  • Taste: Fruity, tangy, layered with both light and dark roast notes.
  • Caffeine: Low.
  • Best Brewing: Pour Over, Blend Roasts, Cold Brew.

Conclusion

Each type tells a different story—Arabica for balance, Robusta for strength, Liberica for rarity, and Excelsa for complexity.
